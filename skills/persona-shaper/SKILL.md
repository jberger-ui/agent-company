# Persona Shaper

This skill converts generic content direction into audience-specific direction.

## Persona dimensions

- goals: what the reader is trying to achieve
- knowledge level: beginner, intermediate, expert, or domain-specific sophistication
- objections or walls: what makes the reader skeptical, stuck, or resistant

## What to produce

- the primary audience definition
- the argument and framing most likely to work for that audience
- the right tone and complexity level
- topics, examples, and proof points to emphasize
- topics or wording to avoid
- one structured brief per routed idea aligned to the persona brief schema

## Guidance

- make the persona operational, not decorative
- prefer concrete objections over marketing stereotypes
- tune complexity to the reader's actual decision-making role
- preserve enough direction that a drafter can write without re-inferring the audience
- prefer business-context objections over vague emotional language

## Do not

- return vague persona fluff
- ignore objections
- collapse multiple routed ideas into one generic persona
